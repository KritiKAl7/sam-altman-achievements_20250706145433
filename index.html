Sam Altman

Born April 22, 1985, Sam Altman is an American entrepreneur, investor, and the CEO of OpenAI. He is a leading figure in the AI boom, known for his visionary leadership and contributions to the tech industry.

Learn More

Early Life & Education

Born in Chicago, Illinois, and grew up in St. Louis, Missouri, Sam Altman showed an early aptitude for computers, receiving his first Apple Macintosh at age eight.

He attended John Burroughs School and later studied computer science at Stanford University for two years before dropping out in 2005 to pursue his entrepreneurial ambitions.

Y Combinator Era

Sam Altman served as President of Y Combinator (YC) from 2014 to 2019. Under his leadership, YC grew significantly, aiming to fund 1,000 companies per year and assisting approximately 1,900 companies in total, including Airbnb, Dropbox, and Reddit.

He introduced YC Continuity, a $700 million equity fund, and Y Combinator Research, a non-profit lab to which he donated $10 million, expanding YC's influence in the startup ecosystem.

OpenAI Leadership

Sam co-founded OpenAI in 2015 with the mission to ensure artificial general intelligence (AGI) benefits all of humanity. He became CEO in 2019, leading the development of groundbreaking models like ChatGPT.

Under his leadership, OpenAI secured billions in funding, including a $10 billion investment from Microsoft. Sam has been a vocal advocate for responsible AI development and regulation, frequently engaging with policymakers worldwide.

Other Ventures & Investments

Sam co-founded Loopt in 2005, a location-based social networking mobile application. He also co-founded venture capital firm Hydrazine Capital.

His impressive investment portfolio includes Helion Energy, Oklo, Retro Biosciences, Reddit, Airbnb, and Stripe, with a total value estimated around $2.8 billion.

Controversies & Challenges

In November 2023, Sam Altman was briefly dismissed as OpenAI CEO, causing significant industry turmoil. However, he was reinstated within days following strong support from employees and investors.

His extensive investments have sparked discussions about potential conflicts of interest with his role at OpenAI, particularly in areas where AI development intersects with his investment portfolio.

Future Outlook

Sam remains focused on ensuring AI benefits all of humanity and believes new hardware is essential for AI's evolution. He continues to invest in initiatives tackling global challenges like energy and health.

Through his leadership at OpenAI and his investment strategy, Sam is positioning himself at the forefront of technological innovation that addresses humanity's most pressing challenges.
